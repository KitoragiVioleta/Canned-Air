# 🥫 Canned Air
> *Breathe the World, Sealed.*

---

## Concept

**Canned Air** is a novelty e-commerce brand that sells sealed cans of air collected from iconic locations around the world. Each can is labelled with its place of origin and tells the story of that destination — giving customers a tangible, humorous, and unexpectedly sentimental piece of somewhere else.

Current collection:
- 🌸 **Tokyo Sakura** — cherry-blossom season air from Ueno Park
- 🧊 **Iceland Glaciers** — crisp arctic air from Vatnajökull
- 🏔️ **Swiss Alps** — high-altitude mountain freshness
- 🍁 **Canadian Rockies** — pine-scented wilderness air
- 🏜️ **Sahara Desert** — warm golden-hour desert breeze
- 🌿 **Amazon Rainforest** — humid, oxygen-rich jungle air

Inspired by **Heso Production** (Japan), who created the original "Heisei era air" souvenir can in 2019 as a way to preserve and gift a moment in time.

**Target audience:** humour gift buyers, travel enthusiasts, collectors, and anyone who has ever said *"I wish I could bottle this moment."*

## Features

- **Sticky navigation** — stays visible on scroll with smooth active-state highlighting
- **Hero section** — full-viewport with dual CTAs ("Shop Now" + "Learn More")
- **Trust badges** — reinforce credibility above the fold
- **Product cards grid** — 6 products displayed in a responsive grid layout
- **"How it works" section** — 3-step explainer for the concept
- **"Why Air?" brand story** — emotional copy section connecting product to experience
- **Customer reviews** — social proof carousel/grid
- **FAQ accordion** — collapsible Q&A to handle common objections
- **Newsletter footer** — email capture with CTA
- **Scroll-reveal animations** — elements animate in as the user scrolls
- **Fully responsive** — optimised for desktop (1440px) and mobile (390px)

## Prompt Engineering Highlights

Two NanoBanana prompt iterations were made to reach the final design:

- **v1** established the basic layout, colour palette, and product grid concept.
- **v2** refined the brief by adding a `product design lead` role, fixing section order to match a conversion-optimised e-commerce flow, and introducing explicit design system constraints: 48px minimum button height, a 12-column / 4-column responsive grid, and an 8pt spacing grid. This iteration produced a significantly more polished and conversion-focused layout.
